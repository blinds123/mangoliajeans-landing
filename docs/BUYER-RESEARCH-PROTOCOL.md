## Phase 4: Copy Hierarchy (Page Structure)

Based on the research above, map copy to page sections:

### Above The Fold (First Screen - 3 Seconds)

**Must Include:**
- #1 Pain or #1 Desire (headline)
- Key differentiator (subheadline)  
- Social proof (reviews count)
- Hero image
- Primary CTA

### Section 2-3: The Hook

**Must Include:**
- #2 Pain point expansion
- "Current vehicle" failure story
- Introduction of "new vehicle"

### Section 4-5: The Transformation

**Must Include:**
- Before/After comparison
- Key benefits (not features)
- #3 and #4 desires addressed

### Section 6-7: Proof & Credibility

**Must Include:**
- Testimonials addressing specific pains
- Trust badges
- Social proof numbers

### Section 8-10: Objection Handling

**Must Include:**
- Common objection 1: ___

- Common objection 2: ___
- How to overcome: ___

### Section 11-12: Urgency & Scarcity

**Must Include:**

- Final CTA

## Phase 6: Copy Generation Rules

### Rule 1: Pain/Desire Priority = Page Position
- Most urgent pain ‚Üí Above the fold
- Least urgent pain ‚Üí Below testimonials

### Rule 2: Headlines Must Pass The "So What?" Test
Every headline must answer: "Why should I care?"

### Rule 3: Features ‚Üí Benefits ‚Üí Outcomes
- ‚ùå "Made with silk lining" (feature)
- ‚úÖ "Feels soft against your skin" (benefit)
- ‚úÖ‚úÖ "Dance all night without a single itch" (outcome)

### Rule 4: Use Their Language
Copy the exact words from reviews, Reddit threads, and customer feedback.

### Rule 5: The "Forehead Slap" Moment
Somewhere in the copy, the reader should think: "That's exactly how I feel!"
